How it works

Set up in two steps. Then forget about it.

No subscription, no cloud service, no company holding your family's data. Ludex runs on your own Google account.

1

Create your self-hosted dashboard

Paste the Ludex script into your Google account. A private Google Sheet becomes your dashboard — your data lives there and nowhere else.

2

Install the agent on your child's computer

A small app runs quietly in the background, tracking which apps are open and for how long. No admin rights needed.

3

That's it

Activity syncs to your dashboard automatically. Set limits per activity, give apps friendly names, and get email alerts when a limit is crossed.

Why transparency works

Kids don't resist limits. They resist arbitrary limits.

When your child can see the same data you see — which apps were open, for how long — there's nothing to argue about. The data is neutral. The conversation shifts from "you've been on that thing for hours" to "we agreed on 60 minutes, let's check."

Over time, kids who know they're being tracked fairly start to self-regulate. That's the point: not to police, but to teach.

Privacy by design

Transparency, not surveillance.

Most parental control tools are spy tools with a friendly face. Ludex is the opposite: it tracks defined activities and time — nothing else. Your child knows exactly what's tracked.

  • Which apps were open and for how long
  • Activity defined by you — games, creative tools, school apps
  • Data stored only in your own Google account
  • No keystrokes, no messages, no content
  • No screenshots
  • No third-party servers
